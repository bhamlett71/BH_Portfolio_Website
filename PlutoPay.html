PlutoPay

About

PlutoPay is a mobile web responsive app that serves as an e-wallet and online banking system that allows users to send and receive in the safest and most efficient way. It is a centralized hub that is directly linked to their checking, savings, credit, and debit accounts without needing multiple banking apps.

Role

Lead UX Designer
UX Researcher

Timeline

5 months

Problem

PlutoPay is an e-wallet mobile web application with a centralized hub for credit, debit, and bank accounts, but users struggle to send and receive in the safest and most efficient way.

Challenge

Our challenge was to create an end-to-end design with the short user flows and multiple security features for people ages 25-45.

My approach was to use various research methods to collect a plethora of data to use to create solutions that uniquely solve users' problem of quickly and securely sending funds virtually to friends and family using a mobile e-wallet platform.

Phase 1: Competitive Analysis

Venmo

PROS

Easy-to-use interface with a flat navigation and very simple layout. There are minimal steps to complete money transactions. Transaction history easily accessible.
CONS

Freezes often. Users are unable to customize contacts list. Purpose for each transaction is not shown on the user's transactions page. Can't track the balance in multiple linked accounts.

Zelle

PROS

App can be downloaded or used straight from a partner banking app. Easy steps and navigation for money transfers. Transaction history is easily accessible.
CONS

Unable to quickly add contacts to contacts list. Limits to amounts of money you can send per day and month. Can not customize contacts list. Not a centralized tracking platform for multiple linked accounts.

I did a comparative analysis of the most used and downloaded e-wallet apps on the currentmarket. I assessed their est features and the challenges that users faced while using the apps. The research showed that users want a simple interface with minimal and secure steps to sed and receive their funds digitally.

Phase 1:Interviews & Surveys

Potential Users…


  • are most concerned with keeping their funds safe and secure
  • want ways to free up their time by minimizing time spent on errands and tasks
  • 65% are very concerned about keeping their personal information safe while using the app and from data breaches
  • enjoy spending time with friends and family at restaurants and feel stressed when they have to spend time to pay bills
  • 95% enjoy using e-wallets to save time and reduce trips to a physical bank
  • 87% do not like to carry cash because it is inconvenient for their busy lifestyles

I wanted to better understand user's behaviors, pain points, and needs to find areas of opportunities to help users spend less time worrying about their funds and enjoy their time with their loved ones.

Phase 1: Personas & Journey Maps

User Personas


Journey Maps


I developed two user personas that reflected the research findings, which showed that users' painpoints were based on their anxiety of safely sending funds to other users. Both personas show that our potential users want simple, quick ways to retrieve their funds and request refunds if they sent money to the wrong user without having to contact customer service. I used the personas to create the journey maps to how users would react to the different phases when completing a task.

Phase 2: Building Solutions

User Flow: Sending Payment

User Flow: Adding a Payment Method

These two user flows follow the motivations and pain points of the user personas. These MVPs are focused on users quickly and securely entering personal information when adding new banking cards/accounts, sending funds using a two-step verification process. This helped to map the structure of each task to provide the minimal amount of steps for a more pleasurable experience.

Phase 2: Prototype & User Testing

Prototype

Feedback

  • “I love the simple design!”
  • “I really like the refund feature. It was hard to find it at first, but I liked it after I found it.”
  • “Automatic and manual are really confusing to me. What does that mean?”
  • “I would love this if the code just enters itself after I get the text message.”

I created low and mid-fidelity wireframes, then completed a round of user testing. The first round of testing showed 100% of users strugged to find the refund feature that was located on the activity page. After making improvements and completing the high-fidelity prototype, I did another round of user testing and received positive, neutral and negative feedback that led me to make improvements more improvements to the prototype such as enlarging print, darkening/lightening colors, and including more icons for better accessibility.

Typography & Color Palette


I wanted PlutoPay to have a calming effect and build trust with users when they send/recieve funds so the color palette is blue and some green and red for contrast. However, when green and red are used, they must align with real-world icons or symbols, for example, a (-) sign for negative funds and (+) for received/refunded funds.
I also wanted the font to be a recognizable and easy-to-read and modern font for our diverse users. This made using Roboto a great choice to use across the app.
